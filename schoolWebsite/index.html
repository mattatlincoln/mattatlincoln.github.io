Research within the School of Mathematics and Physics

School of Mathematics and Physics, University of Lincoln, UK

Welcome

Research within the School of Mathematics and Physics focuses on five key areas: Pure and Applied Mathematics, Astrophysics, Computational Physics and Experimental Nanophysics, as well as contributions from our students.

build Research Areas

Algebra

Pure Mathematics in the School is focused on Group Theory and the Theory of Lie Rings. Our research aims to understand the way groups act on finite and infinite objects, to understand the structure of groups as topological spaces, to achieve greater commutativity of important classes of groups, and to developing novel Lie Rings methods in the study of abstract groups.

Applied Mathematics

Applied mathematical models are used to reveal the properties of nano-structured soft matter, solid/soft matter interface, molecular biophysics and biologically inspired systems.

Found out more
Astrophysics

Our astrophysics research is dealing with one of most complex structures in our Solar system, such as planetary rings, for instance around Saturn.

An emerging direction is also investigation of disks around exoplanets and potential moons formation which could support life.

Computational Physics

The School is home to the Centre for Computational Physics, with research encompassing computational and theoretical physics as well as applied mathematics of nano-structured soft matter, solid/soft matter interface, molecular biophysics and biologically inspired systems.

Nanophysics

The Nanophysics Group brings together expertise in a range of disciplines to focus on various applications of nanotechnology, from renewable energy to nanomedicine.
