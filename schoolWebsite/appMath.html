Research in Applied Mathematics

Welcome

Our research encompasses computational and theoretical physics as well as applied mathematics of nano-structured soft matter, solid/soft matter interface, molecular biophysics and biologically inspired systems. The group aims to understand the self-assembly of such systems, their intricate dynamics and complex patterns, which can aid in the design of smart materials of the future.

mathematics of nano-structured soft matter

Computational physics is one of most exciting and fast developing areas of modern physics, where theoretical physics meets powerful multiprocessor supercomputers. Our field is nano-structured soft matter and biologically inspired systems. We aim to understand their self-assembly and to aid in the design of smart materials of the future.

solid/soft matter interfaces

We analyse and develop simulation tools to investigate the properties of nanocomposite materials includes method development to allow state-of-the-art simulations from the meso to nanoscale.

biologically inspired systems

This direction is a part of research in computer-aided design of bio-mimicking materials. Vesicles are an example, which serves as a model system for biological cells and intracellular compartments. They are also considered as most promising micro-containers in drug delivery research.
